## 一、"How to Get Rich (without getting lucky)" 推文风暴 (2018.05.31)

### 完整推文（39 条）

1. Seek wealth, not money or status. Wealth is having assets that earn while you sleep. Money is how we transfer time and wealth. Status is your place in the social hierarchy.

2. Understand that ethical wealth creation is possible. If you secretly despise wealth, it will elude you.

3. Ignore people playing status games. They gain status by attacking people playing wealth creation games.

4. You're not going to get rich renting out your time. You must own equity — a piece of a business — to gain your financial freedom.

5. You will get rich by giving society what it wants but does not yet know how to get. At scale.

6. Pick an industry where you can play long-term games with long-term people.

7. The Internet has massively broadened the possible space of careers. Most people haven't figured this out yet.

8. Play iterated games. All the returns in life, whether in wealth, relationships, or knowledge, come from compound interest.

9. Pick business partners with high intelligence, energy, and, above all, integrity.

10. Don't partner with cynics and pessimists. Their beliefs are self-fulfilling.

11. Learn to sell. Learn to build. If you can do both, you will be unstoppable.

12. Arm yourself with specific knowledge, accountability, and leverage.

13. Specific knowledge is knowledge that you cannot be trained for. If society can train you, it can train someone else, and replace you.

14. Specific knowledge is found by pursuing your genuine curiosity and passion rather than whatever is hot right now.

15. Building specific knowledge will feel like play to you but will look like work to others.

16. When specific knowledge is taught, it's through apprenticeships, not schools.

17. Specific knowledge is often highly technical or creative. It cannot be outsourced or automated.

18. Embrace accountability, and take business risks under your own name. Society will reward you with responsibility, equity, and leverage.

19. The most accountable people have singular, public, and risky brands: Oprah, Trump, Kanye, Elon.

20. "Give me a lever long enough, and a place to stand, and I will move the earth." — Archimedes

21. Fortunes require leverage. Business leverage comes from capital, people, and products with no marginal cost of replication (code and media).

22. Capital means money. To raise money, apply your specific knowledge, with accountability, and show resulting good judgment.

23. Labor means people working for you. It's the oldest and most fought-over form of leverage. Labor leverage will impress your parents, but don't waste your life chasing it.

24. Capital and labor are permissioned leverage. Everyone is chasing capital, but someone has to give it to you. Everyone is trying to lead, but someone has to follow you.

25. Code and media are permissionless leverage. They're the leverage behind the newly rich. You can create software and media that works for you while you sleep.

26. An army of robots is freely available — it's just packed in data centers for heat and space efficiency. Use it.

27. If you can't code, write books and blogs, record videos and podcasts.

28. Leverage is a force multiplier for your judgment.

29. Judgment requires experience, but can be built faster by learning foundational skills.

30. There is no skill called "business." Avoid business magazines and business classes.

31. Study microeconomics, game theory, psychology, persuasion, ethics, mathematics, and computers.

32. Reading is faster than listening. Doing is faster than watching.

33. You should be too busy to "do coffee," while still keeping an uncluttered calendar.

34. Set and enforce an aspirational personal hourly rate. If fixing a problem will save less than your hourly rate, ignore it. If outsourcing a task will cost less than your hourly rate, outsource it.

35. Work as hard as you can. Even though who you work with and what you work on are more important than how hard you work.

36. Become the best in the world at what you do. Keep redefining what you do until this is true.

37. There are no get rich quick schemes. That's just someone else getting rich off you.

38. Apply specific knowledge, with leverage, and eventually you will get what you deserve.

39. When you're finally wealthy, you'll realize it wasn't what you were seeking in the first place. But that's for another day.

## 二、The Almanack of Naval Ravikant — 核心段落

### Part I: Wealth

#### On Wealth vs Money vs Status

"Wealth is assets that earn while you sleep. Wealth is the factory, the robots that are cranking out things. Wealth is the computer program that's running at night, serving other customers. Wealth is money in the bank that is being reinvested into other assets and into other businesses."

"Money is how we transfer wealth. Money is social credits. It is the ability to have credits and debits of other people's time."

"Status is your ranking in the social hierarchy."

"Status games are always going to exist. There is no way around it, but you should realize that most of the time, when you're trying to create wealth, you're getting attacked by someone else and they're trying to bring you down by competing for status."

#### On Specific Knowledge

"Specific knowledge is found much more by pursuing your innate talents, your genuine curiosity, and your passion. It's not by going to school for whatever is the hottest job; it's not by going into whatever field investors say is the hottest."

"Very often, specific knowledge is at the edge of knowledge. It's also stuff that's only now being figured out or is hard to figure out. If you're not 100 percent into it, somebody else who is 100 percent into it will outperform you. And they won't just outperform you by a little bit — they'll outperform you by a lot because now we're operating the domain of ideas, compound interest really applies and leverage really applies."

"The thing that you are most passionate about isn't 'passion' in the way the world talks about it — the passion that lets you be number one in the world at what you do is obsession."

#### On Leverage

"If I'm a lumberjack with mass-produced saws, I will be ten times better than a lumberjack with an ax. That's leverage. The new forms of leverage are code and media. Code is probably the most powerful form of permissionless leverage."

"Coding, writing books, recording podcasts, tweeting, YouTubing — these kinds of things are permissionless. You don't need anyone's permission to do them."

"The robot army is already here — it's packed away in server farms."

#### On Judgment

"In an age of leverage, one correct decision can win everything. Without leverage, your correct decisions don't matter much. Hard work becomes a lot less useful."

"Wisdom is knowing the long-term consequences of your actions. Wisdom applied to external problems is judgment."

"If you want to be successful, you have to take on a lot of accountability, which means you have to fail a lot. And when you fail, you're going to suffer a lot — unless you have a very strong internal scorecard."

#### On the Principal-Agent Problem

"The principal-agent problem is the single most fundamental problem in microeconomics. If you understand the principal-agent problem, you understand most of what goes wrong in life."

"A principal is an owner; an agent works for the owner. The difference between a founder and an employee is the difference between a principal and an agent."

"If you want to do great things, work for yourself. If you want to get paid a lot, work for others."

#### On Mental Models

"I think the hard thing about these mental models is that they're almost too simple. They're really powerful, but the fact that they're simple means that your ego won't want to admit that something so simple could be so powerful."

"I think being successful is just about not making mistakes. It's not about having correct judgment. It's about avoiding incorrect judgments."

### Part II: Happiness

#### On Happiness as a Skill

"Happiness is there when you remove the sense that something is missing in your life."

"At any given time, when you're walking down the streets, a very small percentage of your brain is focused on the present. The rest is planning the future or regretting the past. This is anxiety."

"A happy person isn't someone who's happy all the time. It's someone who effortlessly interprets events in such a way that they don't lose their innate peace."

"Happiness is a choice. Happiness is a skill. Happiness is a default state."

"The most important trick to being happy is to realize that happiness is a skill you develop and a choice you make. You choose to be happy, and then you work at it. It's just like building muscles."

#### On Desire

"Desire is a contract that you make with yourself to be unhappy until you get what you want."

"I try not to have more than one big desire in my life at any given time, and I also recognize it as the axis of my suffering. I realize that the desire is my suffering."

"One day, I realized with all these people I was jealous of, I couldn't just choose little aspects of their life. I couldn't say I want his body, I want her money, I want his personality. You have to be that person. Do you want to actually be that person with all of their reactions, their desires, their family, their happiness level, their outlook on life, their self-image? If you're not willing to do a wholesale, 24/7, 100 percent swap with who that person is, then there is no point in being jealous."

#### On Peace

"Peace is happiness at rest, and happiness is peace in motion."

"Today, the way we think you get peace is by resolving all your external problems. But there are unlimited external problems, so you are never going to resolve them all."

"The real peace comes from the inside. The real peace comes from letting go of things."

"A calm mind, a fit body, a house full of love. These things cannot be bought. They must be earned."

#### On the Monkey Mind

"The reality is life is a single-player game. You're born alone. You're going to die alone. All of your interpretations are alone. All your memories are alone. You're gone in three generations, and nobody cares. Before you showed up, nobody cared. It's all single player."

"I would combine two things: radical honesty and radical transparency."

"The problem is not the problem. The problem is your attitude about the problem."

#### On Meditation

"Insight meditation is not about making your mind go blank. It's actually the exact opposite. It's about being very aware in every moment."

"Life-hack: when in doubt, squish your brain by going to the gym first thing in the morning."

#### On Reading

"I don't actually read a lot of books. I pick up a lot of books and only get through a few which really speak to me. I'm looking for ideas and things that I didn't know."

"Read what you love until you love to read."

"I don't believe in specific knowledge being taught. I believe in specific knowledge being learned."

"The genuine love for reading itself, when cultivated, is a superpower."

"Reading a book isn't a race—the better the book, the more slowly it should be absorbed."

## 三、Naval Podcast / 播客精华段落

### On Building Yourself

"Escape competition through authenticity. When you're competing with people, it's because you're copying them. Nobody can compete with you on being you."

"The most important skill for getting rich is becoming a perpetual learner."

"I think that the best founders are ones who are very long-term thinkers. Almost all the benefits in life come from compound interest."

"It's better to work on the right thing at 60% effectiveness than the wrong thing at 100%."

### On Decision Making

"If you can't decide, the answer is no."

"If you have two choices to make, and they're relatively equal choices, take the path more difficult and more painful in the short term."

"Simple heuristic: If you're evenly split on a difficult decision, take the path more painful in the short term."

"Easy choices, hard life. Hard choices, easy life."

### On Time

"All the real benefits in life come from compound interest."

"Forty hour workweeks are a relic of the Industrial Age. Knowledge workers function like athletes — train and sprint, then rest and reassess."

"If you're leveraged with media, code, or capital — if somebody can train someone to replace you — then you're playing a dangerous game."

"Set an aspirational hourly rate for yourself. I set my rate at $5,000/hour."

### On Relationships

"You should be working on your product and getting product/market fit. And you should be building your network and figuring out the best people in the world to work with."

"You can't buy love. You can't buy deep friendship. You can't buy health. You can't buy meaning."

"The older the problem, the older the solution."

### On Society

"All the real benefits in life come from compound interest — in relationships, money, technology, everything. It requires consistency over time."

"Free people make free choices. Free choices mean you get unequal outcomes."

"Technology is not only the thing that moves the human race forward, but it's the only thing that ever has. Without technology, we're just monkeys playing in the dirt."

## 五、风格特征速记（用于分析参考）

### 形式特征
- 格言体（aphoristic）：大量 1-2 句话的独立观点
- 推文密度极高：140/280 字符内完成完整论证
- 播客风格：长段落口语，但信息密度高于一般对话
- 对称句式：「X is Y. Z is W.」并列结构
- 类比极多且跨度大

### 主题矩阵
- 财富创造（specific knowledge, leverage, accountability, judgment）
- 幸福哲学（desire as suffering, happiness as skill, peace）
- 学习方法（reading, curiosity, foundational skills）
- 决策框架（heuristics, mental models, long-term thinking）
- 社会观察（status games, technology, freedom）

### 语气特征
- 断言式，极少 hedging
- 禅宗式简洁
- 偶尔引用（Archimedes, Nassim Taleb, Buddha）
- 从不煽情，从不说教口吻
- 自信但不自大——是「想明白了」的安静，不是「我比你强」的炫耀
