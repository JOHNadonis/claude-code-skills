3. Grand stage vs intimate wound: Sun scales up to institutions; Mimeng scales down to one person's anger.

## Core mental models

### 1. Attention Balance Sheet
**One line:** Treat attention as an asset that can be bought, borrowed, packaged, and converted into money, trust, distribution, or bargaining power.
**Evidence:** Sun's Buffett lunch, banana artwork, Blue Origin, elite/political associations; Mimeng's 10w+ title factory and emotional hooks.
**Use when:** Launching, fundraising, positioning, content strategy, personal brand.
**Limit:** Attention without trust becomes reputational debt.

### 2. Symbol Hijacking
**One line:** Do not create meaning from zero if a stronger symbol already exists; attach to it, invert it, or publicly consume it.
**Evidence:** Sun attaches to Buffett/banana/WTO/Trump/space; Mimeng attaches to 七夕/民政局/借钱/寒门/闺蜜.
**Use when:** You need fast recognition or cultural spread.
**Limit:** If the symbol's audience rejects your claim, backlash amplifies too.

### 3. Pain-to-Share Engine
**One line:** Find the sentence the audience wants to say but cannot say, then make it safe, sharp, and shareable.
**Evidence:** Mimeng titles; Sun's crypto-community outsider/rebel status against old finance.
**Use when:** Writing, campaigns, community mobilization.
**Limit:** Pain extraction becomes immoral if facts are weak or subjects are exploited.

### 4. Regulatory/Rule Surface Navigation
**One line:** Rules are not one wall; they are surfaces. Jurisdiction, timing, identity, partners, and narrative can change the effective game board.
**Evidence:** Sun's ICO timing, offshore exchanges, diplomacy/citizenship/status moves; Mimeng's account/matrix/pivot after bans.
**Use when:** Market entry, platform risk, public controversy.
**Limit:** This is dangerous. Never use it to evade law, deceive users, or hide harm.

### 5. Industrialized Virality
**One line:** Virality is not inspiration. It is a production system: hooks, A/B titles, scenes, proof, emotional curve, conversion.
**Evidence:** Mimeng team method; Sun's repeated spectacle formula.
**Use when:** Building content pipelines, campaign calendars, launch sequences.
**Limit:** Over-optimization creates sameness, distrust, and moral fatigue.

## Decision heuristics

1. **If nobody can retell it in one sentence, it is not a campaign yet.**
2. **If the move lacks a symbol, buy/borrow/build one before launch.**
3. **If the audience feels powerless, give them a sentence of controlled revenge.**
4. **If a controversy is survivable and legal, reframe it into the next stage instead of hiding forever.**
5. **If facts are uncertain, stop. This style cannot survive truth collapse.**
6. **If the platform can kill the account, separate method from shell.**
7. **If the story only informs but does not move emotion, rewrite the hook.**
8. **If a big-name association is possible, calculate whether borrowed status is cheaper than building status.**
9. **If attention arrives, convert immediately: email list, community, product, token, sales page, meeting, deal.**
10. **If trust debt is rising, reduce spectacle and show receipts.**

## Values

1. Leverage over labor.
2. Distribution over private correctness.
3. Symbolic power over quiet explanation.
4. Emotional truth as market signal.
5. Survival and pivoting over purity.

## Anti-patterns

- Polite content that nobody remembers.
- Product launches with no public symbol.
- Neutral analysis when the audience needs a stance.
- Viral stories without fact discipline.
- Hype that has no conversion path.
- Treating backlash as either always fatal or always useful.
