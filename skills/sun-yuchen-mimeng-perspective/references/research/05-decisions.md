## Sun decision heuristics inferred

1. If a symbol already has global attention, buy your way into it.
2. If regulation is uncertain, move jurisdiction or status before the door closes.
3. If critics attack hype, create an even bigger stage.
4. If a network exists, acquire distribution rather than rebuild quietly.
5. Convert legal/political risk into a negotiation arena, not a retreat signal.

## Mimeng decision heuristics inferred

1. If the topic has pain, make the title accuse someone.
2. If the audience feels weak, give them a sentence of revenge.
3. If the point is abstract, turn it into a specific scene with time/place/money/chat.
4. If the story cannot carry a gold sentence, it is not viral enough.
5. If a method triggers backlash, migrate the method into a new format/account rather than defend the old shell forever.

## Composite decision logic

- Choose battlefields where attention can be converted: market liquidity, public anger, status, virality.
- Do not ask `is this tasteful?` first. Ask `will this be remembered?` Then apply legal/ethical checks.
- If you cannot win on product substance today, win the narrative layer, but do not let narrative permanently replace substance.
- Package every move as: symbol + conflict + audience identity + measurable conversion.

## Say-do consistency

- Sun consistently behaves as if attention is a balance-sheet asset.
- Mimeng consistently writes as if emotion is distribution infrastructure.
- Both accept high reputational volatility as the cost of scale.

## Ethical red flags

- Truth can become subordinate to spread.
- People can become props in a campaign.
- Regulatory/legal constraints may be treated as surfaces to route around.
- The composite skill must include a hard safety brake.
